Absentee Voting

A voter may qualify to vote by absentee ballot if their duties, occupation, business or vacation requires them to be absent from their county of residence on election day, or if they will be confined by illness, physical disability or some other reason either on a temporary or permanent basis.

Absentee ballots may be obtained by:

  • Writing the Board of Elections (80 State Highway 310, Canton, NY 13617).
  • Printing the appropriate form from below, and submitting that completed application to the BOE office.
  • Calling 315-379-2202.
  • E-mailing your request or print one of the forms that corresponds with your needs.

Qualifications to Vote by Absentee Ballot

  • Absent from St. Lawrence County on election day, or
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to illness or disability, or
  • Unable to appear at the polls due to duties related to primary care of one or more individuals who are ill or physically disabled, or
  • A patient in a Veteransâ€™ Administration Hospital, or
  • Detained in jail awaiting Grand Jury action or confined in prison after conviction for an offense other than a felony.

Permanently Disabled Absentee Ballot

Voters with a permanent illness or physical disability are eligible to vote by absentee ballot. These voters will be put on a permanent list and recieve a ballot for each election in which they are eligible to vote.

Military and Federal Absentee Ballot

Those who are actively in the military, an eligible spouse or dependent, or an activated National Gaurd member on State orders are eligible to vote by Federal Absentee ballot. Also eligible are United States citizens abroad (including those who have never lived in the United States).

Those voting by federal absentee ballot are eligible only for federal elections, unless their last US adress was in St. Lawrence county, and their application indicates an intent to return.

More information is available at the Federal Voting Assistance Program website.
